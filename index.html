The Thesis

Most AI programs stall in the gap between strategy and execution.

Not because the technology failed. Because the organization wasn't ready for what comes next: the integration complexity, the data quality gaps, the absence of anyone who can hold strategy and execution at the same time and keep both honest.

The thing missing for AI is the thing that's been missing all along: the enterprise, business, data, and organizational architecture that creates cohesion across siloed efforts. AI does require new architectural treatment. Orchestration, context delivery, runtime governance for systems that act on their own. These layers are real and they are emerging quickly. They sit on top of and depend on the foundational disciplines underneath, rather than replacing them.

AI as a productivity tool is not the same as AI as an organizational capability. The first is an IT decision. The second is an architecture, leadership, and change problem, which is where the real work lives.

How I Work

A different kind of practice.

Most AI consulting today falls into one of two patterns: engineers who can't credibly advise the business, or strategists who hand off a roadmap and leave. The work I do sits between them, and answers to outcomes, not deliverables.

You work directly with me.

No senior name on the proposal and a junior consultant doing the actual thinking. The trade-off is honest: fewer engagements at a time, full attention on each. When the work needs additional hands, I bring in trusted partners rather than building a bench to staff.

I stay accountable past the deliverable.

Most engagements end with a document. Mine end when the outcome is real. That usually means staying engaged through the parts that get hard.

I build internal capability, not dependency.

The architectural foundation can't be purchased as a deliverable. In mid-market environments especially, engagements should leave the internal team stronger than they were, with the muscle to operate the architecture and evolve it. Capability transfer is part of the work, not an add-on.

I tell you when you're wrong.

Politely, with context, but I tell you. You're not paying me to validate decisions you've already made. You're paying me for perspective that comes from having seen this before.

I don't sell software.

No vendor partnerships. No referral fees. No preferred platforms I'm quietly compensated to recommend. The only thing I'm selling is judgment.
